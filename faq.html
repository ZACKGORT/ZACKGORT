What do you actually do?

A: I design for commerce-focused websites and web products. Think transactional websites {#e-commerce}, and actionable web products {#fintech}.

Essentially, I turn messy product ecosystems into clear, usable experiences. Discovery → IA/taxonomy → prototyping → design systems → ship it.

Strategist? Visual Designer? UX Designer?

A: All of the above. I facilitate like a strategist (problems, goals, constraints) and ship like a designer (flows, components, content, specs).

I have roots as a graphic designer, which, supported by data and fit for purpose, lets me explore full-spectrum user experience solutions.

Where do you add the most value?

A: My can-do and curious creative candor, for starters. Also, my alliterative apropos are handy when making technical jargon, uhm, more “creative” when interfacing with clients and non-technical stakeholders alike.

Also, I am left-handed, which means I naturally operate with a different approach than the masses. Now, assimilate that to how I might function in the business environment.

Approach to Research?

A: Do enough to be impactful early; keep learning as we ship an MVP while continuously iterating and wayfinding.

There is such a thing as “too much research.” Too much qualitative thinking can hinder innovation. Too many agencies fear improvisation, but that’s exactly what most unique business requirements—and creative types—require.

“A story is something that constantly unfolds.” — Quentin Tarantino

Design System Philosophy?

A: Practical first. Systems should be canonical, implementation-friendly, and maintenance-friendly {you know; by using tokens, providing usage guidance, and quickly validating design in a code environment, etc}.

Short Timelines?

A: Isn’t everyone working on a short timeline? I worked at a boutique agency where we shipped an entire end-to-end e-commerce build on a 12-week engagement; whereas at a big-four tech consulting agency for a large bank, I’d say the only reason we had a year-long contract was to give a 50+ person team something to do.

Sarcasm aside, a large part of my job as an experience designer is working with the PM to identify the lowest-friction wayfinding through key project checkpoints {discovery, research, client and user interviews, prototype iteration, feature development, presentations & approvals, reworks, etc}.

Tools and Stacks?

A: Whatever keeps the team fast and honest; and frankly, working with whatever they prefer.

Figma for design + docs, Notion/Jira/Miro/Trello/etc for project/task upkeep, and building with modern codebases (React + Tailwind).

Communication Style & Process?

A: I prefer to convey plainly and directly. I take a lot of notes. I ask a lot of questions. I take the time to dive deep into the client’s POV.

Identify the problem → Propose creative/technical solutions → Support this through science {data/research/etc} → Iterate → Validate → Ship it.

Background?

A: My career started with startups {e-commerce, lead generation}. I took an interest in coding and became an interactive designer at a full-service design agency. From there, it’s been a mix of industries spanning e-commerce to iOS apps to financial products.

Photographer/Retoucher → Graphic Designer → Web Designer → Interactive Designer → UX Designer → Senior UX Designer → UX Manager → Senior Product Designer

How would we work together?

A: Trust. Trust that if you need something, or the team needs something, or the client needs something, I am readily available to support the need.

I just ask that I be fed well.

Availability & Engagements?

A: Senior IC/lead, fractional product/design management, or short, surgical engagements to unblock discovery, IA, or systemization. Flexible on timezone and cadence, though I’d prefer an enticing city to relocate to.
